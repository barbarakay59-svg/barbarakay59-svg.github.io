Poppop's World

By Barb Kay

Dec. 4, 2025

A week before New York City shut down due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we learned that my grandfather, Joel “Poppop” Friedman, was diagnosed with dementia. In the nearly six years since, his memory is sporadic, if he has it at all some days. The National Library of Medicine has reported that lockdown and social isolation may have significantly increased the likelihood, and degree, of symptoms associated with Alzheimer's disease. “In one study, caregivers of AD patients described increased hallucinatory experiences by the patients during lockdown, compared to before lockdown,” the National Library of Medicine wrote."Another study indicated that during the COVID-19 pandemic, AD patients experienced a decline in cognitive abilities, deterioration of neuropsychiatric symptoms, and more sleep disturbances than before the pandemic.”
